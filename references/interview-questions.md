# SPCC Plan Interview Questions

Complete reference for Phase 2 of the SPCC Plan Drafter skill.
Ask all groups. Groups may be combined into a single message but keep them labeled.
Where a user answers with "?" or "unknown," record the gap and flag it in Phase 4.

---

## Group A — Facility Identity

1. **Facility name** (legal name of the facility/site)
2. **Facility street address**, city, state, ZIP
3. **Facility county** and **EPA Region** (if known)
4. **Owner name** (company or individual legally responsible)
5. **Owner mailing address** (if different from facility)
6. **Owner phone and email**
7. **Facility operator name** (if different from owner)
8. **Operator phone and email**
9. **SPCC Plan Coordinator name and title** (person responsible for plan implementation)
10. **SIC code or primary business activity** (e.g., manufacturing, agriculture, power generation)
11. **EPA Facility ID or Facility Registry Service (FRS) ID** (if known; OK to leave blank)
12. **Is this a new plan or an amendment to an existing plan?**
    - If amendment: what is the current plan revision number and date?
    - What changed that triggered the amendment?
13. **Plan preparer name, title, company, and contact info** (may be same as coordinator)

## Group B — Oil Storage Inventory

Collect the following for EACH oil-containing container or equipment item on site.
Present as a table or numbered list. One row per container.

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Container ID | User-assigned tag (e.g., AST-1, DR-01, TR-A) |
| Container type | AST (fixed roof, floating roof, cone bottom, etc.) / Drum / IBC / Tote / Transformer / Day tank / Lube oil reservoir / Bulk transfer vehicle / Other |
| Capacity | Gallons (for transformers, use gallons of dielectric fluid) |
| Actual contents | Oil type: petroleum (diesel, gasoline, crude, used oil, lubricant, hydraulic fluid) or non-petroleum (vegetable oil, animal fat, synthetic) |
| Location on site | Building name, tank farm, yard area — reference a site map if provided |
| Year installed or approximate age | |
| Material of construction | Steel, fiberglass, HDPE, etc. |
| Condition | Good / Fair / Poor / Unknown |
| In service? | Yes / No / Seasonal |

**Follow-up questions:**
- Do any containers store NON-petroleum oils (vegetable, animal, synthetic)?
  *These are still regulated under Part 112 but use §112.9 provisions.*
- Are any containers completely buried underground?
  *USTs are generally excluded but must be noted in the plan.*
- Are there any containers that are temporarily out of service but not yet removed?
- Any mobile/portable containers (drums, totes) that move around the site?
  *If yes: what is the maximum number stored at any one time and where?*
- Any containers exempt from SPCC (capacity < 55 gallons, non-oil contents)?

**Totals to calculate after receiving answers:**
- Total aboveground oil storage capacity (gallons)
- Largest single aboveground container (gallons)
- Total petroleum oil storage
- Total non-petroleum oil storage

## Group C — Secondary Containment

For each container or group of containers:

1. **Type of secondary containment**: Berm / Dike / Vault / Double-wall tank / Containment pad / Spill pallet / None
2. **Containment capacity** (gallons or cubic feet)
3. **Does the containment hold ≥ 110% of the largest single container it serves?**
   *(Required by §112.7(c) unless impracticability is claimed)*
4. **Material and condition of containment structure**
5. **Is the containment area covered/roofed or open to precipitation?**
6. **How is accumulated stormwater managed?**
   (Manually pumped out? Gravity drain with valve? Automatic drain?)
7. **Is the drain valve normally open or closed?**
   *(Should be normally closed per best management practice)*
8. **For any container WITHOUT adequate secondary containment:**
   - Why is it impracticable? (location, operations, cost?)
   - What equivalent environmental protection measures are in place?
   *(Impracticability claims require PE certification for Full Plans; must be documented)*

## Group D — Discharge Pathway & Water Proximity

1. **Name of the nearest navigable water body** (river, creek, lake, bay, wetland, storm drain to navigable waters)
2. **Approximate distance from the facility's oil storage areas to that water body** (feet or miles)
3. **General topography**: Does the site slope toward the water body?
4. **Stormwater drainage description**: Where does site runoff go? Storm sewer? Retention pond? Open drainage ditch? Direct to waterway?
5. **Are there any stormwater retention ponds, oil-water separators, or berms between storage areas and the site boundary?**
6. **What is the estimated time for a spill at the largest container to reach a navigable water body?** (hours, days — estimate OK)
7. **Are there any catch basins, floor drains, or trenches on site?** Where do they discharge?
8. **Site map available?** (Ask user to upload or describe layout)

## Group E — Operations & Transfer

1. **Are oil transfers performed on site?** (Loading/unloading from tank trucks, railcars, pipelines, vessels?)
2. **Frequency of transfers** (daily, weekly, monthly)
3. **Transfer method**: Pump / gravity / pressure
4. **Are transfers performed from a fixed unloading area or variable locations?**
5. **Is a dedicated drip containment pad or drip tray used during transfers?**
6. **Are anti-siphon devices, overfill protection, and/or high-level alarms installed on receiving tanks?**
7. **Are bulk transfer vehicles (tank trucks) on site?** If yes:
   - Capacity
   - Where are they parked when loaded?
   - Are they included in the total storage inventory?
8. **Day tanks**: If present, are they fed from a larger AST? What is the day tank capacity and location?
9. **Are any oils processed or used in manufacturing operations?** (e.g., quench oil, cutting oil, hydraulic presses)
10. **Are there any pipelines on site that carry oil?** (Above or below ground)
    - Length, diameter, product, age
    - Cathodic protection on buried lines?

## Group F — Inspection & Maintenance History

1. **Are oil storage containers currently being inspected?**
2. **Inspection frequency**: Daily walk-around / Weekly / Monthly / Annually / Not currently
3. **Who conducts inspections?** (Designated employee, third-party inspector)
4. **Are inspection records maintained?** If yes, for how long?
5. **Has any container ever failed an integrity test?** If yes, describe.
6. **Are gauges, vent valves, and liquid level devices tested regularly?**
7. **For ASTs ≥ 660 gallons: has a formal integrity test (e.g., API 653) been performed?**
   - Date of last test
   - Result
8. **Any known leaks, seeps, or staining around containers or piping?**
9. **Age of oldest container on site** — has a brittle fracture evaluation been performed?
   *(Required for field-constructed ASTs that have been relocated — §112.7(i))*

## Group G — Security

1. **Is the facility fenced?** (Perimeter fence type and condition)
2. **Are entry gates locked when facility is unattended?**
3. **Is lighting adequate at storage areas for nighttime security?**
4. **Are starter controls for oil transfer pumps locked or otherwise secured?**
5. **Are loading/unloading connections secured with caps or locks when not in use?**
6. **Are warning signs posted at oil storage areas?**
7. **Any security cameras covering storage areas?**
8. **Is there 24/7 staffing or is the facility unattended at certain times?**

---

## Group H — Personnel & Training

1. **Name and title of the SPCC Plan Coordinator / Oil Spill Response Coordinator**
2. **How many employees handle oil (transfers, inspections, maintenance)?**
3. **Is there a documented oil spill response training program?**
4. **Training frequency**: Annual / Periodic / One-time at hire / None
5. **Are training records maintained?**
6. **Are spill response procedures posted at oil storage/transfer areas?**
7. **Is there an emergency contact list (fire department, USEPA, state agency, cleanup contractor) posted on site?**
8. **Is there an Oil Spill Response Organization (OSRO) or cleanup contractor under contract?**
   - Name of contractor, contact info
9. **Is there any spill response equipment on site?** (Absorbents, booms, containment drums)
   - Location and quantity

## Group I — Spill & Incident History

*(Required for tier determination. Must be answered accurately.)*

1. **Has the facility had any oil discharge to navigable waters or adjoining shorelines in the past 3 years?**
   - If YES: date, volume, cause, was it reported?
2. **Has any single discharge exceeded 1,000 gallons?**
3. **Have there been two or more discharges each exceeding 42 gallons within any 12-month period?**
4. **Any near-miss incidents (spills contained on-site, overfills caught before reaching water)?**
5. **Any enforcement actions, notices of violation, or consent orders from EPA or state environmental agency related to oil storage or spills?**
6. **Any past SPCC plans in place at this facility?** If yes:
   - Date of last plan
   - Date of last PE certification or self-certification
   - Has the plan ever been amended?
