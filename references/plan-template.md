## SECTION 1 — FACILITY DESCRIPTION (§112.7 Preamble)

**Required content:**
1.1 Facility name, address, and owner/operator contact information
1.2 SPCC Plan Coordinator name, title, phone, and email
1.3 Facility SIC code and description of primary operations
1.4 Physical description of the site: total acreage, terrain, general layout
1.5 Description of oil handling activities: types of oil used, stored, or processed
1.6 Regulatory tier determination:
  - State the determined tier (Tier I QF / Tier II QF / Full Plan)
  - Total aboveground oil storage capacity (gallons)
  - Largest single aboveground container (gallons)
  - Basis for tier determination with reference to §112.3(g) or §112.3(d)
1.7 List of applicable regulations (40 CFR Part 112; state environmental regulations if any)
1.8 Plan history: date of original plan, prior amendments, date of last PE certification

## SECTION 2 — OIL STORAGE CONTAINER INVENTORY (§112.7; §112.8(c))

**Required content:**
2.1 Complete container inventory table with the following columns:
  - Container ID
  - Container type (AST, drum, transformer, day tank, etc.)
  - Nominal capacity (gallons)
  - Oil type / contents
  - Location on site
  - Year installed / age
  - In service (Y/N)
  - Secondary containment provided (Y/N) — reference containment ID

2.2 Summary totals:
  - Total aboveground oil storage capacity: ___ gallons
  - Total petroleum oil storage: ___ gallons
  - Total non-petroleum oil storage: ___ gallons
  - Number of containers subject to SPCC: ___

2.3 For USTs on site: list separately with note that USTs are excluded from SPCC
    applicability calculation per §112.1(d)(4), but note their presence.

2.4 For transformers: note dielectric fluid type (mineral oil, silicone, ester-based)
    and confirm whether fluid is listed as "non-PCB" per TSCA requirements.

## SECTION 3 — SITE DRAINAGE AND DISCHARGE PREDICTION (§112.7(b))

**Required content:**
3.1 Description of site topography and general drainage direction
3.2 Name and location of the nearest navigable water body or storm drain to navigable waters
3.3 Distance from storage areas to the nearest water body
3.4 Estimated direction, rate, and total quantity of discharge in a worst-case scenario:
  - Worst-case spill = full contents of largest single container: ___ gallons
  - Estimated direction of flow: [describe based on topography]
  - Estimated time to reach navigable waters: [hours/days]
3.5 Description of all drainage pathways: storm sewers, ditches, floor drains, catch basins
3.6 Description of any natural or engineered barriers between storage areas and water body
3.7 Site map reference (attach as Appendix A; note location of all containers, containment
    structures, drainage features, and nearest water bodies)

## SECTION 4 — SECONDARY CONTAINMENT AND DIVERSIONARY STRUCTURES (§112.7(c)-(d))

**Required content:**
4.1 For each containment system:
  - Containment ID and description
  - Containers served
  - Containment type (berm, dike, vault, double-wall, pad, pallet)
  - Containment capacity (gallons)
  - 110% capacity calculation: [Largest container volume] × 1.10 = [Required capacity]
  - Actual capacity vs. required capacity — does it comply? (Y/N)
  - Material and condition
  - Open to precipitation? (Y/N) — if yes, describe stormwater management
  - Drain type and normal position (open/closed)

4.2 For any impracticability claims (§112.7(d)):
  - Identify the container(s) for which secondary containment is impracticable
  - State the basis for impracticability (operational, geographic, or engineering)
  - Describe the equivalent environmental protection measures in place
  - ⚠️ NOTE: Impracticability claims in Full Plans require PE certification of the
    equivalent measures

4.3 For mobile/portable containers without fixed containment:
  - Describe portable containment (spill pallets, drip trays) used during operations
  - Describe storage location and any area containment

## SECTION 5 — BULK STORAGE CONTAINERS — INSPECTION AND INTEGRITY (§112.8(c) / §112.12(c))

**Required content:**
5.1 For each AST ≥ 660 gallons:
  - Date of last formal integrity test (API 653 or equivalent)
  - Result of test
  - Testing frequency going forward
  - Description of leak detection method (visual inspection, sensors, inventory reconciliation)

5.2 For all containers:
  - Overfill protection measures: high-level alarms, audible alerts, automatic shutoffs
  - Corrosion protection: coating type, cathodic protection (for buried piping)
  - Compatibility of container material with stored product

5.3 Inspection frequency table:

| Container Type | Inspection Frequency | Inspector |
|---------------|---------------------|-----------|
| ASTs | [Specify — recommend monthly visual] | [Name/role] |
| Drums/portables | [At each use or weekly] | [Name/role] |
| Transformers | [Monthly visual + annual fluid test] | [Name/role] |
| Day tanks | [Daily/weekly] | [Name/role] |
| Transfer piping | [Monthly] | [Name/role] |

5.4 Record-keeping: Describe where inspection records are kept and retention period
    (minimum 3 years required — §112.7(e)(8))

5.5 Brittle fracture evaluation: For any field-constructed AST that has been relocated or
    has undergone major repair, document whether a brittle fracture evaluation has been
    conducted per §112.7(i).

## SECTION 6 — TRANSFER OPERATIONS, PUMPING, AND IN-PLANT PIPING (§112.7(h) / §112.8(d))

**Required content:**
6.1 Description of all oil transfer operations on site (loading, unloading, internal transfers)
6.2 For each transfer point:
  - Location
  - Method (pump, gravity, pressure)
  - Hose/fitting management: inspection before each use, visual drip check
  - Containment at transfer point (drip pad, drip tray, portable sump)
  - Coupling type and condition
6.3 Aboveground piping:
  - Material, age, and condition
  - Supports and anchoring
  - Inspection frequency
6.4 Buried/underground piping:
  - Material, age
  - Cathodic protection type (impressed current or sacrificial anode)
  - Last cathodic protection test date and result
  - Leak detection method
6.5 Valves: location of shutoff valves, locked open/closed status, labeled?
6.6 Briefing of personnel responsible for transfer operations (reference Section 9)

## SECTION 7 — FACILITY DRAINAGE MANAGEMENT (§112.7(e))

**Required content:**

  - What test or visual check is performed to confirm liquid is uncontaminated?
  - How is contaminated liquid removed and disposed?
7.3 Describe any oil-water separators on site: location, maintenance frequency
7.4 Describe any stormwater retention ponds or basins: capacity, outlet description
7.5 Describe floor drain systems in any buildings with oil storage or operations
7.6 Describe any engineered barriers (berms, curbing) at site perimeter or drainage points

## SECTION 10 — SPILL RESPONSE PROCEDURES (§112.7(a)(3)-(4))

**Required content:**
10.1 Immediate response steps (numbered list):
  1. Stop the source of the spill if safe to do so
  2. Alert the SPCC Plan Coordinator and facility management
  3. Activate emergency contacts if needed (fire, HAZMAT)
  4. Report to National Response Center (1-800-424-8802) if discharge reaches navigable
     waters or may be harmful to public health
  5. Report to state environmental agency per state requirements
  6. Deploy spill response equipment
  7. Contain and recover spilled material
  8. Document the incident

10.2 Spill response equipment on site:
  - Type, quantity, and location of absorbents, booms, containment drums, pumps
  - Procedure for replenishing used equipment

10.3 Reportable quantity thresholds:
  - Federal: any discharge to navigable waters that may be harmful (§112.1(b)) —
    includes discharges > 1,000 gallons or two discharges > 42 gal in 12 months
  - State: [insert state-specific threshold and reporting agency]

10.4 Spill reporting record form reference (attach as Appendix C)

10.5 Post-spill corrective action: amend the SPCC plan if discharge reveals a deficiency

## SECTION 12 — PLAN AMENDMENT AND REVIEW PROCEDURES (§112.5)

**Required content:**
